ABOUT Art360 Kibera

Art 360 Kibera is a collective of young artists born and bred in Kibera slums Nairobi, Kenya. The artists have been part of the Art platform since they were young, trained and later becoming professionals in their specific art interess. The artists aim to revoke, empower and create a sense of urgency in today's societal issue including geoeconomics and politics, extra judicial killings etc. Art 360 Kibera projects have been published in different magazines globally including the National Geographic. Our main massages at this time is to create awareness and provide detailed factual information on to the people of Kibera slums through creation of murals.

Mavyuva Maingi Benard

Am a self tought artist from kibera Nairobi I started art when I was young by doing sketches doing craft and making toy cars Ever since I started my art I used to it as a way to pass time but latter I decided to pursue it as a career I decided to look for a place with artist where we can share ideas and that is when I joined Art 360 Kibera in May 2019 I do all kinds of art but I specialize in abstract paintings and landscapes I also teach art not only drawing but also other forms of art

Francis Omondi Otieno

I started art when I was in primary school it was just as a hobby and having fun by being creative as a child and making myself toys it grew to the point where I realized it was my talent When I finished High school I decided to make it my profession My main style is called falk abstract which talks more about traditional lifestyle I also venture in other forms of art like paper montage banana fiber montage I also do graffiti and murals I have also been  an art teacher in several schools in Nairobi and other places like Machako where I teach them not only how to draw but other forms of art as well

Faith Atieno Owino

I Started art 10 years doing ago when I was in  high school at that moment I was doing it for fun until I finished High school and at that point I started pursuing it as a career I do expression abstract and I draw my inspiration from women social life in the slums I also mentor children on all kinds of art and give them the necessary skills to create their own arts My biggest Achievement is when I got invited by European Union to do a mural In Bulgaria and Denmark
